<p><p>Recycled polyester (rPET) is derived from post-consumer plastic waste, primarily PET bottles, and is used to create sustainable textiles. The market for rPET can be segmented into two main types: polyester filament and polyester staple fiber. Polyester filament is produced as continuous strands, ideal for applications like apparel and industrial textiles. In contrast, polyester staple fiber is made from shorter fibers and is commonly used in non-woven fabrics, insulation, and filling materials, contributing to environmental sustainability.</p></p>
