<p><p>Waste-to-Energy (WTE) refers to converting waste materials into usable energy, primarily through incineration and other technologies. Incineration involves burning waste at high temperatures to generate heat, which is then converted into electricity or steam. Other WTE methods include anaerobic digestion, which breaks down organic waste to produce biogas, and gasification, which transforms waste into syngas for energy production. These methods reduce landfill use, lower greenhouse gas emissions, and generate renewable energy, contributing to waste management and sustainability efforts.</p></p>
