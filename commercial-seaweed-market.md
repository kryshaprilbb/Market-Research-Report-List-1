<p><p>The commercial seaweed market is segmented into three main types: red, brown, and green seaweeds, each with distinct uses and nutritional profiles. Red seaweeds, like agar and carrageenan producers, are primarily utilized in food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. Brown seaweeds, such as kombu and wakame, are popular in culinary applications and for their health benefits. Green seaweeds, including spirulina and chlorella, are known for their high protein content and are often used in health supplements and smoothies. Each type serves diverse markets.</p></p>
