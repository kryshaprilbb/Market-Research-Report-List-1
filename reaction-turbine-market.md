<p><p>The reaction turbine market is primarily segmented into axial flow and radial flow types. Axial flow turbines feature a design where water flows parallel to the turbine's axis, effectively generating power in a streamlined manner, typically seen in larger installations. In contrast, radial flow turbines direct water outward from the center, promoting efficient energy conversion, particularly in hydroelectric plants with varying water levels. Each type caters to specific applications based on efficiency, capacity, and installation requirements within the energy sector.</p></p>
