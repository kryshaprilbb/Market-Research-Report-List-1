<p><p>Thin film solar panels utilize various materials to convert sunlight into electricity. Amorphous Silicon (a-Si) is lightweight and offers flexibility but has lower efficiency. Cadmium Telluride (CdTe) is cost-effective and performs well in low-light conditions. Copper Indium Gallium Selenide (CIS/CIGS) combines high efficiency with versatility, making it suitable for various applications. Other types may include organic or perovskite films. Each type presents unique advantages and challenges, influencing adoption based on efficiency, cost, and application suitability in the renewable energy market.</p></p>
